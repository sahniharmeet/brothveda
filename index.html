Organic apple cider vinegar is added at the start of the simmer — not for flavour, but for function. The acetic acid in ACV helps draw minerals out of the bone matrix into the broth. Calcium, magnesium, phosphorus, and potassium are locked inside the bone structure. ACV acts as a mild acid that breaks down the bone matrix faster, releasing more minerals into the liquid during the 18-hour simmer. By the time Rasa is ready, the vinegar has cooked off completely — you will not taste it. What remains is a significantly more mineral-dense broth.
